AAMCHI MUMBAI

The Koli, an aboriginal tribe of fishermen, were the earliest known inhabitants of present-day Mumbai, though Paleolithic stone implements found at Kandivli, in Greater Mumbai, indicate that the area has been inhabited by humans for hundreds of thousands of years. The city was a centre of maritime trade with Persia and Egypt in 1000 BCE. It was part of Ashoka’s empire in the 3rd century BCE, and in the 2nd century CE it was known as Heptanesia to Ptolemy, the ancient Egyptian astronomer and geographer of Greek descent.

In 1348 the island was conquered by invading Muslim forces and became part of the kingdom of Gujarat. A Portuguese attempt to conquer Mahim failed in 1507, but in 1534 Sultan Bahādur Shah, the ruler of Gujarat, ceded the island to the Portuguese. In 1661 it came under British control as part of the marriage settlement between King Charles II and Catherine of Braganza, sister of the king of Portugal. The crown ceded it to the East India Company in 1668.

Mumbai continued to grow and prosper in the 21st century, in large part because of advances in the technology sector. By the second decade of the century the population of Greater Mumbai was approaching 20 million. The city’s infrastructure was improved considerably with the construction of new highways and bridges, expansion of port facilities, and the inauguration of new public-transit systems. Overcrowding, traffic congestion, environmental pollution, and widespread poverty, however, remained major ongoing problems.

PLACES TO VISIT

Gateway of India

It is one of the best place to visit in Mumbai, was built in 1924 by George Willet to honor the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Mumbai. Visit this place early morning or late evening hours to enjoy the cool breeze of the sea sans the crowd.

Red Carpet Wax Museum

It exhibits lifelike wax figures of prominent icons from various fields including science, politics, sports and world cinema like Barack Obama, Michael Jackson, Harry Potter and many more. So, take selfies with your favorite icon, post it.

Elephanta Caves

Take a tour to this UNESCO World Heritage Site in Mumbai and get wonderstruck by the massive rock-cut caves that date back to several centuries. It has five caves dedicated to Hindu god Shiva and two caves depicting Buddhist architectures.

KidZania

Kidzania is an indoor amusement park made exclusively for kids with a unique concept of role-playing in a real city-like environment. Here, kids are required to perform a ‘job’ as a part of their role-playing and they get paid in the form of kidzo money as an encouragement.

Taraporewala Aquarium

Touted as India’s oldest aquarium, this place houses a variety of marine and freshwater fishes including coral fishes from the Lakshadweep Islands. It also has an Oceanarium that gives you the feel of walking under the sea with exotic fishes floating above you.

Juhu Beach

Nothing can be more romantic than watching the crimson sun and the luminous orange sky on a breezy beach. Juhu Beach is one such spectacular place in Mumbai, often visited by couples who need a little romantic escape from the humdrum of city life.

BEST PLACES TO EAT

Yugo Sushi

Butter Chicken Sushi Roll is what the Bombay Roll at Yugo Sushi is better known as. If you aren’t one to find ‘paan’ macarons and gulab jamun cheesecakes blasphemous, you’ll love the slightly dry butter chicken wrapped in nori sheet with pickled vegetables.

Ram and Shyam

Ram & Shyam is nothing more than a food cart in Santacruz and sets up only by 3 pm, but the crowd that gathers close to their opening hour, make this cart feel much larger than life. It’s tough to pick a favourite, so we suggest you eat the Ragda Pattice.

Kepchaki Momos

Mumbai doesn’t have nearly as many varieties of momos or momo sellers when compared to Delhi. But what we lack in quantity, we make up for with quality and the authentic Nepalese momos at Kepchaki’s stall on Carter Road are a delicious example of that.

Suzette

There are plenty of savoury crepes, sandwiches and salads to choose from at Suzette, but it’s their Melted Belgian Chocolate Crepe that’ll keep you coming back for more. Its located at Nariman Point and costs 1200 bucks for a meal of two.

Pizza by the Bay

Pizza By The Bay was one of the first restaurants in Mumbai to be dedicated entirely to pizzas. A recent addition to their menu is the Flaming Pizza where they pour a flaming shot of vodka or rum over the pizza to turn it into a flambee of sorts.

Ladu Samrat

Mumbai’s favourite snack, the vada pav, comes to the rescue when you want something quick. Even though Ladoo Samrat serves a host of Maharashtrian delicacies, nothing beats their vada pav, a perfect blend of spice and crunch.

MAIN FESTIVALS AND FAIRS

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is the main festival of Mumbai among Hindus in Mumbai. In 1893, freedom fighter Lokmanya Tilak transformed this festival into a public event to gain India’s unity among castes to rebel against the British rule. This week-long festivals has prayers, hymns and performances in almost every lane.

Krishna Janmashtami

According to Hindu Mythology, Krishna Janmashtami commemorates the birthday of Lord Krishna. Of all the Hindu festivals here, Mumbai janmashtami festival holds an important place. The ISKCON temple gets festive with prayers until midnight. Children are dressed as Krishna and Radha.

Kala Ghoda Arts Fest

The Kala Ghoda Arts Festival is a cultural festival, nine days long. The festival commences on the first Saturday of February and closes on the second Sunday in February. It is one of the best upcoming festivals in Mumbai. It is the celebration of various different forms of dance, art, music and theatre.

India Art Fest

Mumbai is one of the cities to host the premier INDIA ART FESTIVAL. The event is a huge art fest in Mumbai where art galleries, artists, art dealers, interior designers, architects and art buyers come under one roof. It is a platform for artists to showcase their works.

Golden Citizen Festival

Started in 2012, it aims at ending global poverty and Chris Martin, Coldplay’s lead vocalist is the curator for this massive event. The event has most of who’s who of Bollywood and Hollywood artists like Coldplay, Jay Z, Demi Lovato, and India’s legend A.R.Rahman.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

The city was earlier called Bombay. The name was changed by an act of the Indian parliament in 1997. The reason for this change was that in two of the local languages, Marathi and Gujarati, the city has long been called Mumbai. It is believed that the name comes from the name of one of the old Koli goddesses, Mumba Devi, a temple to whom now stands in Bhuleshwar.

The island city of Mumbai, spread linearly along the Arabian Sea on the West Coast of India, is linked to the mainland by several bridges. Because of its deep natural harbour, it has been an important shipping and trading centre and the commercial capital of the country.

25 is the minimum age for consumption of alcohol in Mumbai.

Mumbai is generally considered relatively safe for tourists. However, petty crimes like pickpocketing or bag snatching are common. It’s advisable to always be cautious of your surroundings and avoid visiting isolated areas.

Mumbai is best between the months of October and March.

The temperature rarely drops below 25° C (77 F). So, bring clothes that are light-coloured and breathable. Cotton clothes are the best.
